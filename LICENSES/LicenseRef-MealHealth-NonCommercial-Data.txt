MealHealth bundled-data licence (non-commercial)
================================================

The CSV files under ``src/mealhealth/data/`` are PROCESSED, ADAPTED and
SUBSTANTIALLY REDUCED derivatives of third-party datasets. They are not the raw
source data: they contain only the minimum quantities (dose-response curves,
per-country baseline food-group intakes and calories, cause/age death rates,
population age structure and abridged life tables) needed to evaluate this
package's health-impact formulas.

Upstream sources and their terms:

1. IHME Global Burden of Disease (GBD) — relative-risk dose-response curves and
   cause-specific mortality rates.
   Licence: IHME Free-of-Charge Non-commercial User Agreement
   (see LicenseRef-IHME-NonCommercial.txt). Non-commercial use only; the raw
   data may not be redistributed. Only adapted, aggregated derivatives are
   bundled here.

2. GDD-IA (M. Springmann et al., in preparation) — baseline per-country
   food-group intakes, the processed/unprocessed red-meat split, and total
   dietary energy, as constructed by the GLADE project.
   Licence: planned public release under CC-BY-4.0; shared with this project's
   author by permission ahead of that release. Only a reduced, derived product
   is bundled here. Attribute to M. Springmann et al. (GDD-IA).

3. UN World Population Prospects (WPP) — population age structure and abridged
   life tables (survivorship and remaining life expectancy).
   Licence: Creative Commons Attribution 3.0 IGO (CC BY 3.0 IGO).

Because the bundled data inherits the most restrictive of these upstream terms,
the bundled data — and therefore this package AS A WHOLE when distributed with
it — is provided for NON-COMMERCIAL research, teaching and private study only,
WITH ATTRIBUTION to the sources above. This restriction stands independently of
the GPL-3.0-or-later licence that applies to the package's source code: the GPL
covers the code, not these data files, and does not grant any commercial-use or
unrestricted-redistribution rights over them.

No warranty is provided. When publishing results, cite GBD (IHME), GDD-IA
(M. Springmann et al.), UN WPP, and GLADE, as described in docs/data_sources.md.
